Dung Beetle

What is a dung beetle?

A dung beetle is an insect that can roll up balls of its own poop and use it for survival purposes. A single dung beetle can produce feces 250 times the weight of it in just one day.

Types of dung beetles

There are 3 types of dung beetles. The rollers make the balls of dung that all of the dung beetles eat or use as breeding chambers. Tunnelers bury all dung that they can get their hands on. Dwellers just sit around and do nothing except slowly eat the dung shelter they decided to crawl into.

Uses of dung beetles

As gross as it may sound, dung from dung beetles can be used as soil to grow plants. Tunnelers can protect livestock in farms by burying the livestocks dung, which can serve as a hazard to them. When dead, the dung beetle can be used in Chinese medicine, and it can cure 10 different diseases.
